Objective

This tutorial will guide you through creating a 3-slide presentation for Tawara University as required by Task 6. We will use the provided source files, `M21STORESPRES.rtf` and `M21CHART.csv`, to build the presentation in Microsoft PowerPoint.

Step 1: Setting Up the Master Slide

  • Open PowerPoint and create a new blank presentation. You will need 3 slides in total.
  • Go to the View tab and click on Slide Master.
  • Select the main parent master slide (the top slide in the left-hand pane).
  • Go to the Insert tab, click Header & Footer.
  • In the dialog box, check the Slide number box.
  • Check the Footer box and type your Name, Centre Number, and Candidate Number into the text field.
  • Click Apply to All.
  • Now, adjust the positions: move the slide number placeholder to the top right corner.
  • Move the footer placeholder with your details to the bottom left corner.
  • Click Close Master View from the Slide Master tab.

Step 2: Creating the Title Slide (Slide 1)

  • Make sure Slide 1 has a Title Slide layout.
  • Open the `M21STORESPRES.rtf` file. Copy the first line, "Tawara Science Stores".
  • Paste this into the title placeholder on Slide 1.
  • In the subtitle placeholder, type Presented by: followed by your name.

Step 3: Importing Content (Slide 2)

  • Go to Slide 2. Use a Title and Content layout.
  • Set the title of the slide to Stores Information.
  • In the `M21STORESPRES.rtf` file, copy all the text from "Departments served" to the end.
  • Paste this text into the content box on Slide 2.
  • Format the text as a bulleted list, demoting items to create sub-bullets as needed.

Step 4: Creating the Bar Chart (Slide 3)

  • Go to Slide 3. Use a Title and Content layout and set the title to Product Analysis.
  • Open `M21CHART.csv` in a spreadsheet program.
  • Select the data and insert a Clustered Column chart.
  • Format the chart: set the title to Number of Products Supplied, add axis titles (Number of Products for vertical, Supplier for horizontal), add data labels on top of the bars, and remove the legend.
  • Copy the finished chart and paste it onto Slide 3 in PowerPoint.

Step 5: Finalizing and Printing

  • On Slide 3, add the speaker note: This chart shows the diversity of our supplier base.
  • Print the presentation as handouts with 2 slides per page.
  • Print only Slide 3 as a full page slide.

In the IGCSE ICT (0417) syllabus, presentation skills are a core component of Paper 2. The exam tests your ability to create a professional presentation with a consistent corporate identity, clear information, and integrated objects like charts.

Practice Exercise with Solution

Task:

Create a short, 2-slide presentation about a school event.
1. The master slide must show your name in the bottom-right and automatic slide numbers in the top-left.
2. Slide 1 should be a Title Slide with the event name and your name.
3. Slide 2 should use a Title and Content layout, with a title and at least four bullet points describing the event.

Click to see Step-by-Step Solution
  1. Open PowerPoint: Start a new blank presentation.
  2. Edit Master Slide: Go to View > Slide Master. Select the top parent slide.
  3. Insert Footer & Slide Number: Go to Insert > Header & Footer. Check "Slide number" and "Footer". In the footer box, type your name. Click "Apply to All".
  4. Reposition Placeholders: Drag the slide number placeholder (`<#>` or `(#)`) to the top-left corner. Drag the footer placeholder with your name to the bottom-right.
  5. Close Master View: Click the "Close Master View" button.
  6. Create Slide 1: On the first slide, which should already be a Title Slide layout, enter the event name (e.g., "Annual Sports Day") as the title and your name as the subtitle.
  7. Create Slide 2: Add a new slide (Home > New Slide). Change its layout to "Title and Content".
  8. Add Content to Slide 2: Enter a title (e.g., "Event Details"). In the content box, add at least four bullet points (e.g., "- Date: Friday, October 25th", "- Time: 9:00 AM - 3:00 PM", "- Location: School Sports Field", "- Events: 100m Sprint, Relay Race, Long Jump").

  • Formatting: Modifying the chart's appearance for clarity.
  • Integration: Copying and pasting the chart into other documents.

Practice Exercise with Solution

Task:

In a spreadsheet program, enter the following data for monthly sales:
- January: 150
- February: 180
- March: 220
Create a vertical bar (column) chart to display this data. Set the chart title to "Q1 Sales". Label the vertical axis "Sales ($)" and the horizontal axis "Month". Add the value of each bar as a data label on top of the bar.

Click to see Step-by-Step Solution
  1. Open Spreadsheet & Enter Data: Open a program like Excel. In cell A1, type "Month". In B1, type "Sales". Enter the months and values in the rows below.
  2. Select Data: Click and drag to select the range A1:B4.
  3. Insert Chart: Go to the Insert tab and click on the "Column or Bar Chart" icon. Choose the first option, "Clustered Column".
  4. Set Chart Title: Click on the default "Chart Title" text on the chart itself. Delete the existing text and type "Q1 Sales".
  5. Add Axis Titles: Click on the chart. A `+` icon will appear on the right. Click it, then check the box for "Axis Titles".
  6. Edit Axis Titles: Click on the new "Axis Title" placeholder on the vertical (Y) axis and type "Sales ($)". Click the horizontal (X) axis title and type "Month".
  7. Add Data Labels: Click the `+` icon again and check the box for "Data Labels". This will add the values on top of each bar.
