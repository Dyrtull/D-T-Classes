🔄 What is a Transducer?

A transducer is an electronic device that converts energy from one form to another.

Transducer Senses Converts To
LDR Light level Changing resistance
Thermistor (NTC) Temperature Changing resistance

💬 They don't generate electricity. They simply change their resistance—and that resistance change is what we measure using the potential divider we already know.

👁️ The LDR: The Garden's Eye

An LDR's resistance decreases as light increases—and vice versa. It's like a shy person: in a bright crowd, they open up. In the dark, they close off.

LDR Behaviour Table

Light Condition LDR Resistance
Bright sunlight ~120Ω (very low)
Dim indoor light ~10kΩ (medium)
Completely covered (dark) Up to 10MΩ (very high)

💬 The Key Pattern: More light → Lower resistance. Less light → Higher resistance.

Why the LDR Changes Resistance

The LDR contains a semiconductor material (usually cadmium sulphide) whose electrical properties change when exposed to light. Photons of light knock electrons loose, increasing conductivity and DECREASING resistance.

This means:

  • More photons = more free electrons = lower resistance
  • Fewer photons (darkness) = fewer free electrons = higher resistance
  • The change happens instantly (in milliseconds)

🌡️ The Thermistor: The Garden's Skin

A thermistor is a resistor whose value changes with temperature. The NTC type (Negative Temperature Coefficient) is what we use: As temperature increases, resistance decreases.

Thermistor Behaviour Table

Temperature Condition NTC Thermistor Resistance
Cold (~10°C) High resistance
Room temp (~25°C) Medium resistance
Hot (~40°C) Low resistance

💬 The Key Pattern: More heat → Lower resistance. Less heat → Higher resistance. (Same as LDR with light!)

The Medellín Thermal Cycle

Temperatures in Medellín range from ~12°C at night to ~28°C during the day. Your thermistor will respond to these variations to control watering and cooling.

Technical Specs:

  • Hotter temp = more thermal energy = more free electrons = lower resistance
  • The relationship is nonlinear (not a straight line)
  • Response time: ~5-10 seconds for significant change

⚡ The Live Sensor Formula

When we place an LDR as R₁ and a fixed resistor as R₂ in a potential divider, Vout changes automatically.

Vout = (R₂ / (RLDR + R₂)) × Vin

Real-World Scenarios (R₂ = 10kΩ, Vin = 9V)

🌞 Bright Sunlight

LDR = 120Ω

Vout = (10k / (120 + 10k)) × 9

8.89V
🌙 After Dark

LDR = 50kΩ

Vout = (10k / (50k + 10k)) × 9

1.5V

⭐ THE CRITICAL INSIGHT ⭐

As darkness falls, Vout falls from 8.89V down to 1.5V. The transistor (Week 7) switches ON at 0.6V. That threshold is your garden's "bedtime alarm."
