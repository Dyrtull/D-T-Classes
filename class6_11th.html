Week 6: The Effects of IT & ICT Applications

For Classes 11A, 11B, and 11C

Section 1: The Microprocessor Effect (Time: 20 minutes)

Microprocessors: The Brains Behind the Tech

Many modern devices, from your smartphone to your home heating system, are controlled by microprocessors. These tiny circuits act as the brain, executing instructions. But this technology has both positive and negative effects on our lives and society.

In-Class Exercise: "Case Study: The Smart Home"

Objective: To analyze the positive and negative effects of microprocessor-controlled devices in a home setting.

Instructions:

  1. Group up. Each group will be assigned a "smart home" device, such as a smart heating system, a security alarm, or a home entertainment system.
  2. Brainstorm. Using the provided documents and your own knowledge, list the positive and negative impacts of your assigned device. Consider its effects on:
    • Lifestyle: How does it change daily tasks?
    • Leisure: How does it affect your free time?
    • Physical Fitness: Does it make you more or less active?
    • Social Interactions: How does it influence how you connect with others?
    • Security: Are there risks of cybercriminal activity?
  3. Present. Prepare a 2-3 minute presentation to another class group. Be sure to use a visual aid (e.g., a simple drawing or a list on the board).

Section 2: Health & Well-being (Time: 25 minutes)

Healthy Habits for a Digital World

Prolonged IT use can lead to health issues like Repetitive Strain Injury (RSI), back and neck problems, eye strain, and headaches. But these can be prevented with simple strategies.

Objective: To design and implement a healthy, ergonomic workstation.

Instructions:

  1. Work in pairs. You and your partner are "Ergonomics Consultants." Your task is to identify and correct poor posture and workstation setups in the classroom.
  2. Consult. Take a few minutes to observe your partner's current setup. Note down any potential issues, such as a hunched back, strained neck, or incorrect arm position.
  3. Implement. Using the provided documents and class materials, offer advice to your partner. Help them adjust their chair, monitor, keyboard, and mouse for an ideal setup. Consider using a footrest or wrist support if needed.
  4. Create a Guide. As a pair, create a small "Healthy Habits" guide. It should include:
    • A drawing or description of an ideal ergonomic workstation.
    • At least three tips for preventing health issues. (e.g., take regular breaks, use an anti-glare screen filter).
    • A brief explanation of what causes one of the health issues (e.g., eye strain from staring at a screen for too long).

Section 4: Audience Appreciation (Time: 15 minutes)

Who Are You Talking To?

The best ICT solutions are created with the end-user in mind. This is called audience appreciation. How you present information should change depending on who your audience is.

In-Class Exercise: "Instruction Manual Remake" üìù

Objective: To demonstrate how to tailor information for different audiences.

Instructions:

  1. Pair up. You will be assigned a simple ICT device, like a smart thermostat, a fitness tracker, or a digital camera.
  2. Define the Audience. Your group will create two different "instruction manuals" for the same device, but for two very different audiences:
    • Audience 1: A tech-savvy teenager who is familiar with apps and modern interfaces.
    • Audience 2: An elderly person who has limited experience with technology.
  3. Tailor the Content. Consider the following as you create your manuals:
    • Language: Should it be formal, informal, or use a lot of technical jargon?
    • Visuals: How many images or diagrams should you include? What kind of visuals would be most helpful?
    • Steps: How detailed should the instructions be? Should you break down complex steps into smaller, simpler ones?
  4. Present & Discuss. Share a key page or section from each of your manuals. As a class, discuss the differences and why these changes are necessary to ensure the solution is effective for its intended audience.
