üî® Assembly Steps - Pasos de Ensamblaje

1 Prepare Your Workspace

Prepara tu Espacio de Trabajo

  • Clear table or desk surface
  • Organize all components in groups
  • Have tools ready and accessible
  • Keep instruction sheet visible

üí° Pro Tip:

Use small containers or paper cups to organize screws and small parts so they don't roll away!

2 Mount the Motor to Chassis

Monta el Motor al Chasis

  • Locate the motor mounting holes on your base plate (usually rear center)
  • Position yellow motor so output shaft faces toward the rear
  • Insert M3 screws through chassis into motor mounting holes
  • Tighten nuts on opposite side - not too tight! (you'll crack the acrylic)
  • Verify motor is secure but can still be adjusted slightly
Check: Motor shaft should point toward the back of the car. If it points sideways, your car will have trouble moving straight!

3 Install Axles and Wheels

Instala los Ejes y las Ruedas

  • Locate the axle slots (rectangular openings on sides of chassis)
  • Insert axle rods through slots - they should slide freely but not wobble
  • If axles are too loose, add small piece of tape to increase diameter
  • Push wheels onto axle ends until they click or are firmly seated
  • Spin wheels - they should rotate smoothly without rubbing chassis
  • All four wheels should touch the ground at same height

üí° Troubleshooting:

Car veers to one side? Check that wheels are aligned straight and rotate freely. Bent axle or misaligned wheel is common cause.

4 Position and Secure Battery

Posiciona y Asegura la Bater√≠a

  • Find flat area on chassis for battery (usually center, near motor)
  • Important: Battery should be low and centered for stability
  • Use velcro strips, zip ties, or tape to secure battery firmly
  • Battery must NOT move when car is shaken - secure it well!
  • Leave wires accessible for connection to TP4056
Weight Distribution: Battery is heaviest component. Position it centrally over the axles for best balance and straight-line performance.

5 Mount TP4056 Charging Module

Monta el M√≥dulo de Carga TP4056

  • Position TP4056 near battery (close connections = less resistance)
  • Module should be mounted so LED indicators are visible from top/side
  • Use M3 screws through mounting holes OR use double-sided tape
  • Ensure module is stable - it will be connected/disconnected for charging
  • Identify ports:
    • IN+ / IN- (input from solar panel)
    • B+ / B- (battery connection)
    • OUT+ / OUT- (output to motor circuit)

6 Connect Battery to TP4056

Conecta la Bater√≠a al TP4056

  • CRITICAL: Match polarity correctly! Red = + (positive), Black = - (negative)
  • Connect battery RED (+) wire to TP4056 B+
  • Connect battery BLACK (-) wire to TP4056 B-
  • Connections should be firm - gently tug to verify
  • If using screw terminals: tighten screws after inserting wires
  • If pre-soldered: wires may have JST connectors - push firmly until they click

‚ö†Ô∏è STOP! Double-Check:

  • Red battery wire goes to B+ (positive)
  • Black battery wire goes to B- (negative)
  • If you reverse these, you could damage the module!
  • Before proceeding, ask teacher to verify connections

7 Attach Solar Panel Mount

Adjunta el Soporte del Panel Solar

  • If using separate solar mount plate: attach to chassis with side brackets using M3 screws
  • Mount should elevate solar panel above other components
  • Angle can be adjustable (best for experiments!) or fixed at ~30-45¬∞
  • Ensure mount is stable - solar panel will add weight
  • Leave access to TP4056 for wire connections

8 Mount Solar Panel

Monta el Panel Solar

  • Position solar panel on mount facing upward
  • Secure with screws through panel mounting holes (if available)
  • OR use strong double-sided tape if no mounting holes
  • Panel should be stable but positioned for easy adjustment
  • Wire connections should reach TP4056 without pulling tight
Solar Panel Orientation: The dark/blue side captures sunlight. This should face UP. The back side (usually white/silver) faces down toward chassis.

9 Connect Solar Panel to TP4056

Conecta el Panel Solar al TP4056

  • Connect solar panel RED (+) wire to TP4056 IN+
  • Connect solar panel BLACK (-) wire to TP4056 IN-
  • Verify polarity markings on solar panel (often printed on back)
  • Secure connections - they'll be connected/disconnected during experiments

üí° Testing Tip:

After connecting solar panel, hold car under bright light or sunlight. The RED LED on TP4056 should light up, indicating charging is happening!

10 Install the Switch

Instala el Interruptor

  • Position switch where it's easy to reach (side or top of car)
  • Mount switch using screws or strong adhesive
  • Switch will control power flow from TP4056 to motor
  • Label switch positions: ON and OFF (use marker or label maker)

11 Wire the Motor Circuit

Cablea el Circuito del Motor

This is the final electrical connection - follow carefully!

Circuit Diagram / Diagrama del Circuito:

TP4056 OUT+ (red wire) ‚Üí Switch middle terminal Switch output terminal ‚Üí Motor + (red/positive wire) Motor - (black/negative wire) ‚Üí TP4056 OUT- (black wire)
  • Connection 1: TP4056 OUT+ to switch middle/common terminal
  • Connection 2: Switch output terminal to motor positive (+) wire
  • Connection 3: Motor negative (-) wire to TP4056 OUT-
  • Verify all connections are secure
  • Wires should be organized - not tangled or loose
Understanding the Switch: The switch acts like a gate. When ON, it allows electricity to flow from battery ‚Üí motor. When OFF, the gate closes and motor stops. This lets you control when car moves without disconnecting wires!

12 Final Testing & Verification

Prueba Final y Verificaci√≥n

‚úÖ Pre-Test Checklist:

  • All screws tightened (but not over-tightened)
  • Battery firmly secured and won't shift
  • All wires connected to correct terminals
  • No loose wires touching each other
  • Wheels spin freely without rubbing
  • Solar panel facing upward
  • TP4056 LEDs visible
  • Switch accessible and labeled

üîã Charging Test:

  1. Ensure switch is OFF
  2. Place car in bright sunlight (or under strong lamp)
  3. Observe TP4056: RED LED should light up (charging)
  4. Wait 10-15 minutes
  5. If battery is full, BLUE LED will light (or red will turn off)
  6. If no LEDs light: Check solar panel connections

üöó Motor Test:

  1. Place car on flat surface (NOT edge of table!)
  2. Flip switch to ON position
  3. Motor should run and wheels should turn
  4. Car should move forward in relatively straight line
  5. If motor doesn't run:
    • Check battery has charge (try charging longer)
    • Verify all connections secure
    • Check switch is working
    • Ask teacher to test with multimeter
  6. If car veers badly: adjust wheel alignment or check motor mounting
