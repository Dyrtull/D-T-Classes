Concept Exploration

Class 2 Lever: The Power Lifter

Definition: The LOAD is in the MIDDLE, between the fulcrum and the effort.

Think of a Wheelbarrow. The pivot (wheel) is at the end. The heavy dirt (load) is in the middle. You lift handles (effort) at the other end.

Mechanical Advantage (MA)

$$MA = \frac{\text{Distance to Effort}}{\text{Distance to Load}}$$

Because the Effort arm is always longer than the Load arm in Class 2, MA is always > 1. This means it multiplies your force!


Class 3 Lever: The Speedster

Definition: The EFFORT is in the MIDDLE.

Think of Tweezers or your Arm. The pivot is at one end. You squeeze in the middle. The object is at the tip.

Why use it?

The MA is usually less than 1 (Mechanical Disadvantage). You need more force than the object weighs. However, you gain distance and speed.


Linkages: Connected Levers

A linkage is a system of levers connected by pivots. Changing the length of one bar changes the motion of the whole system.

4-Bar Linkage: Consists of a fixed frame, a crank (rotates), a rocker (oscillates), and a coupler. Observe how the geometry determines the path.

Worked Example: The Human Arm (Class 3)

Scenario: Elbow to Bicep = 5cm. Elbow to Hand = 35cm.

$$MA = \frac{5}{35} = 0.14$$

This is a mechanical disadvantage. You need roughly 7x the force to lift the object, but your hand moves 7x faster than your muscle contracts!
