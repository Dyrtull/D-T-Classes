Step 1: Open and Save the File

Open the file `m25calc.csv` in your spreadsheet software. Since it's a CSV file, it will likely open in a plain format without any special formatting.

Immediately save the file in a format that supports formulas and formatting, such as an Excel Workbook (`.xlsx`). It is a good practice to save it with a new file name, such as `m25calc_solution.xlsx`.

Step 2: Add Your Details and Replicate the Layout

In cells `A3` and `A4`, enter your name and candidate number. These details are important for identifying your work.

Replicate the spreadsheet layout as it appears in the source file, merging cells A1 and B1 to make them one cell and centring the text. Your spreadsheet should look like the image below after this step.

Step 3: Enter and Format Formulas

This is the main part of the task. You will need to calculate the plaster area for the ceiling and the walls. The instructions in the PDF are to:

  • Calculate the plaster area for the ceiling.
  • Calculate the plaster area for the walls.

Here are the formulas you need to enter into the specified cells: 


                        
                        =B6*B5

                        
                        =(2*B5*B4)+(2*B6*B4)-(B8*B7)-(B11*B10)

Enter the formula for the ceiling area in cell `B19`. The ceiling area is the length multiplied by the width of the room. This corresponds to cells `B6` and `B5`.

Enter the formula for the wall area in cell `B20`. The wall area is the total area of the four walls minus the area of the door and any windows. The total wall area is calculated as $(2 \times \text{Room Width} \times \text{Room Height}) + (2 \times \text{Room Length} \times \text{Room Height})$. This corresponds to $(2 \times B5 \times B4) + (2 \times B6 \times B4)$. From this, you subtract the door area $(B8 \times B7)$ and the window area $(B11 \times B10)$.

Make sure to format the cells containing the formulas to display only one decimal place as required by the task.

Step 4: Hide and Print

After entering all the formulas and formatting, you need to prepare the spreadsheet for printing according to the instructions. The requirements are:

  • Hide the rows from 1 to 18.
  • Ensure the printout fits on a single page.
  • The printout should be in portrait orientation.
  • Row and column headings are not displayed.
  • The contents of all cells are fully visible.

To hide rows, select rows 1 through 18, right-click, and choose 'Hide'. You may need to adjust print settings in your software to ensure it fits on a single portrait page and that row and column headings are turned off before printing.

Step 5: Save and Print the Final Version

Save your final spreadsheet. It is now ready to be printed and submitted. Make sure to check the print preview one last time to confirm all the requirements are met.
