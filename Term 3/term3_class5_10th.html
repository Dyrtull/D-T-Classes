Key Quantities

Quantity Symbol Unit Definition
Voltage V Volt (V) Electrical pressure pushing electrons.
Current I Ampere (A) The rate of electron flow.
Resistance R Ohm (Ω) Opposition to current flow.

Circuit Rules

Ohm's Law

V = I × R

Use the triangle! Cover the one you want to find.

Series vs. Parallel

  • Series: Resistance adds up ($R_T = R1 + R2$). Current is the same everywhere.
  • Parallel: Resistance decreases ($R_T = \frac{R1 \times R2}{R1 + R2}$). Voltage is the same across branches.

The Potential Divider

Used for sensors (LDRs, Thermistors). It splits the supply voltage based on the ratio of resistances.

Formula:

$$V_{out} = V_{in} \times \frac{R_2}{R_1 + R_2}$$

Where $R_2$ is usually the resistor you are measuring across (often the bottom one).

⚡ The 7.4V Adjustment

⚠️ IMPORTANT EXAM UPDATE

Walkthrough: Calculating Current with 7.4V

Scenario: You have a 100Ω resistor connected to the new 7.4V supply.

Step 1: Identify values. $V = 7.4V$, $R = 100\Omega$.

Step 2: Choose Formula. $I = \frac{V}{R}$.

Step 3: Calculate.

$$I = \frac{7.4}{100} = 0.074 A$$

Step 4: Convert to mA. $0.074 \times 1000 = 74 mA$.

📖 Exam Vocabulary

Potential Difference
Another name for Voltage. The difference in electrical energy between two points in a circuit.
Transducer
An electronic device that converts energy from one form to another (e.g., LDR converts light to resistance).
Tolerance
The permitted percentage error of a resistor (e.g., Gold band = ±5%).
Nominal Resistance
The stated value of a resistor based on its color code.
Direct Current (DC)
Current that flows in one direction only (like from our 7.4V battery).
